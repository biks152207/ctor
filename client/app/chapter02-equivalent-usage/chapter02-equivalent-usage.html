Why Do We Need Equivalent Systems?

Without the equivalent systems it would be difficult to compare the effect of different forces because, at first glance, we can only see the forces and not the hidden moments. With equivalent systems, we show both the forces and the moments that the tooth will receive at the center of resistance and, therefore, we can evaluate how much displacement and rotation each force produces.

For example, if we apply a force of 100 cN and another force of 50 cN on a tooth, we can summarize the displacement and rotation effect of each force at the center of resistance and conclude if the final effect is additive or subtractive and the final displacement and rotation that the tooth will express (Figure 2.6).

     Figure 2.6. The final effect of two different forces applied opposite to each other at different distances from center of resistance can be evaluated using an equivalent system. In this case it is easier to understand the resultant force and moment created.

Understanding the equivalent system helps us clinically to foresee the effect of our mechanotherapy and what to expect to see at the patient’s next visit (Figure 2.7).

     Figure 2.7. Equivalent system showing the response to different forces with the same point of application (molar tube) in the molar crown. Click on each tooth view to see the force and moment created at the center of resistance.

Note that the equivalent system should be analyzed in every dimension (Figure 2.8), because the magnitude and direction of moments in each dimension can be different.
